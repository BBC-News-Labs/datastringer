Datastringer - A tool for hacker-journos

What we're going to do

Now is time to get a new data source in. I propose we use Police.uk again, as it has a set of questions which guarantee us some good pegs for our stories.
You can ask the API to give you several informations about your local police force, ranging from its members to planned events, and set priorities for our bobbies.

"That's not even data, man!"

Yeah, so what? Datastringer is not only good at crunching numbers according to your criteria. Its first role is to query, read, check, and compare for you a big set of things you would access with your computer. And to do that transparently, without needing you. That's computer-assisted reporting.

Anyway, the job here is simpler than before. We just need to know which local force you're interested in following. Datastringer will then detect the changes and alert you when a new police officer joins the force, or when an event is planned (and you should attend, free muffins!), or when new priorities are set.

Seems easy, right?

We will just need a GPS location. As before, go to the next page to get that easily.
