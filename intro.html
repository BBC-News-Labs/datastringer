Datastringer - A tool for hacker-journos

What we're going to do

We are going to help you configure a data source, based on Police.uk.

Police.uk has an API, i.e. a computer we can send questions to, and get answers in return. One of the questions we can ask Police.uk is

"How many crimes, and what crimes, were commited in this area, during this month?"
That's a rather cool question, eh? You can get these stats super easily for any place in the UK. If you are a local journalist, you will probably want to write stories about crime in your area. Well, I'd like to.

However, the raw numbers in themselves don't quite tell a story yet.

Knowing that 62 bicycles were stolen last month in Leicester is rather useless without context:

Is it more than before? Is this number on the rise?
To add context to these numbers, I have added some tweaking under the hood for this example. Not only will it fetch this month crime, it will also have a look at several months before, to compare how this month's crime numbers compare to a x-months average.

Ready?

We will need a set of dates, and a GPS location. The next page will help you get hold of these.
